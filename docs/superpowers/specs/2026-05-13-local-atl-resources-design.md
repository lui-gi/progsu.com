**Date:** 2026-05-13

## Overview

A curated list of 15–20 Atlanta-area tech companies worth targeting as a GSU CS student. The guide is ordered by tier priority: ProgSU exec companies first, then FAANG with ATL presence, then Fortune 500. Each entry is concise and actionable.

## Structure

### Intro (2–3 sentences)
Explain why local ATL companies are strategic: shorter recruiting cycles, easier in-person networking, and strong full-time conversion rates for interns.

### Company List

A single flat ordered list of 15–20 companies sorted by tier:

1. **ProgSU Exec tier** — companies where current ProgSU execs work (listed first)
2. **FAANG in ATL** — FAANG/big tech companies with Atlanta offices
3. **Fortune 500 in ATL** — remaining major ATL-area employers

**Confirmed exec-tier companies (ATL presence verified):**
- Norfolk Southern
- BlackRock
- Uber
- FanDuel
- Cox Automotive
- Intuit
- IBM
- The Home Depot

**Excluded (no significant ATL presence):**
- GitHub (SF-based, remote-first)
- DV Trading (Chicago)
- The Assembly DC (Washington DC)
- Apple (Cupertino)

### Per-entry format

Each company entry contains:
- **Company name** (bold)
- Inline tier badge: `[ProgSU Exec]`, `[FAANG]`, or `[Fortune 500]`
- One sentence: what the company does + why it's worth targeting as an ATL student
- Hiring timeline in season + months format (e.g. "Summer internships open Jan–Mar")

### Structure approach

**Option B** (chosen): Single flat ordered list with inline tier badges. No section breaks between tiers — the priority order conveys importance, and badges provide context without fragmenting the page.

## Content decisions

- Quality over quantity: 15–20 companies max, no padding
- Exec-tier companies get `[ProgSU Exec]` badge to signal insider advantage
- Hiring timeline is freeform text (season + months), not a structured table
- No external links required — this is a reference list, not a link directory
